Rainbow Six Siege's New Event is a Huge Hit with the Community

Golden-Gun Mode has reignited the Siege Community, Well Done Ubisoft

Rainbow Six Siege has had various limited-time events in the past, with the possibility of more in the future. One such event that players really enjoyed was the "Six Invitational 2021" event that occurred earlier this year. The event introduced a fresh game mode named "Golden Gun" where players had to eliminate one another using a one-hit-kill golden gun, as well as several new cosmetics such as weapon skins, headgear, and uniforms. The Six Invitational 2021 event was well-liked by the Rainbow Six Siege community, leading to an increase in the number of players during its duration. In general, these limited-time events provide an exciting way to keep players interested and engaged in the game, while also offering an opportunity to introduce new content and features.

Has Warzone 2.0 started to roll down hill with its player count at an all-time low?

Will W2 player count continue to drop or will it be revived?

Unsurprisingly, MW2 and Warzone 2 were most popular during the first weeks after the release, so in November 2022. According to Steam, about 224,000 people played the game on average in November, with an all-time peak of 488,897 players.

About six months later, the average number of players decreased significantly to less than 90,000 in the last 30 days, with a peak of around 157,000 players. This indicates that the game lost over half of its player base compared to the launch weeks. It's worth noting that the player count on Battle.net, Xbox, and PlayStation would have to be combined to determine the overall number of players, but it's difficult because Sony and Microsoft don't offer a detailed player count monitoring option like Steam does. However, since the content is identical on all platforms and both Warzone 2 and MW2 are crossplay, it's probable that the player curve will be comparable across platforms. This sheds light on why Modern Warfare 2 and Warzone 2 have experienced a decrease in their player count.

In addition to the typical drop in player and viewer numbers that usually occurs shortly after a game's release, many players were dissatisfied with some of the changes made to Warzone 2. Some even went so far as to claim that the game was already dead. Moreover, the second season of the game was delayed by a whole month, and not every player may have been willing to wait an extra four weeks to finally experience some new content. Ultimately, the decrease in the number of people playing Call of Duty MW2 and Warzone 2 is probably due to a combination of various factors mentioned in this article.
